Man dies after suffering medical emergency on Eisenhower


A 58-year-old south suburban man died Tuesday after he suffered a medical emergency while driving on the Eisenhower Expressway in the western suburbs.
Randall Williams of Dolton was driving eastbound on I-290 near Mannheim Road about 3:30 p.m. when he suffered a medical emergency and pulled over, according to Illinois State Police and the Cook County medical examiner’s office.
He was unresponsive in the car when an ambulance arrived at the scene, and was taken to Loyola University Medical Center, where he was pronounced dead at 4:02 p.m., authorities said.
No one else was in the car and no one else was hurt, according to police.
An autopsy is scheduled for Wednesday.

SUV smashes into front entrance of Streeterville hospital

An SUV smashed through the front entrance of Northwestern Medicine’s Prentice Women’s Hospital in Streeterville early Wednesday.
About 2:45 a.m., a valet accidentally accelerated instead of braking the vehicle and crashed into the doorway of the hospital at 250 E. Superior, police News Affairs Officer Hector Alfaro said.
The accident caused minor property damage and no one was hurt, Alfaro said.
No citations were expected to be issued.

Three Oak Forest businesses cited for underage liquor sales

Three businesses in south suburban Oak Forest were cited last week for selling alcohol to minors, state authorities said.
The Illinois Liquor Control Commission and the Oak Forest Police Department conducted compliance checks on 19 businesses in Oak Forest on Thursday, Feb. 19, according to a statement from the ILCC.
Three businesses were issued citations after they failed the check:
– Cindy’s Pub, 5230 W. 159th St.;
– Circle K, 5600 W. 159th St.;
– Oak Forest Bowl, 15240 S. Cicero Ave.
The 16 other businesses passed the compliance check by refusing to sell liquor to the ILCC’s underage participant, according to the statement. None of the businesses visited had previously failed a compliance check.
